# Product

## Name

Heading

## Register

product

## Users

CPL aspirants, student pilots, aviation academy students, and flight training candidates preparing for DGCA written exams. Typically 18–30, studying in concentrated sessions between training flights. Primary task: work through practice questions by subject, track weak areas, and close knowledge gaps before the exam date.

Secondary segment: instructors reviewing student progress via admin surfaces.

## Product Purpose

A premium DGCA CPL exam preparation platform. Students work through a question bank by subject (met, nav, systems, regs), run timed mock exams, use viva flashcards, and track their progress. Success: a student leaves the exam hall with a pass because the app showed them the right gaps at the right time with minimum cognitive friction.

## Primary Goals

1. Maximize learning efficiency
2. Reduce cognitive load
3. Improve retention
4. Increase study consistency
5. Deliver a premium aviation learning experience

## Brand Personality

Precise, calm, trusted, professional. Luxury cockpit aesthetics: authoritative, no noise, every element earns its place. The interface should feel like it was built by someone who actually holds a licence and knows what's at stake. Editorial typography, strong hierarchy, purposeful motion.

## Anti-references

- **Duolingo / gamified edtech**: streaks as confetti, XP explosions, cartoon mascots, neon reward screens. Aviation exam prep is serious; the product should feel like it.
- **Generic SaaS cream**: warm beige hero, gradient text, hero-metric templates, uppercase tracked eyebrows above every section. Indistinguishable from 500 other 2024 products.
- **Byju's / Unacademy clutter**: feature overload, competing CTAs, low-density chaos. The domain is already dense; the UI must be cleaner than the content.
- **Over-decoration**: excessive gradients, nested cards, visual noise, weak contrast.

## Design Principles

1. **Clarity over decoration.** Exam content is dense. Every visual element not helping comprehension is competing with it.
2. **Earned authority.** Aviation is safety-critical. The interface must feel reliable and precise, not playful or trendy. Trust is built through consistency, not personality injections.
3. **Progress is the primary signal.** The product's core job: show students where they stand and what to do next. Progress indicators, gap analysis, and pacing are the hero UI, not decorative metrics.
4. **One coherent system.** Quiz, Today dashboard, Modules, and Admin should share a vocabulary. A button that looks different in two places makes one of them wrong.
5. **Dark mode is first-class.** Students study at night and in low-light cockpit environments. Dark mode is not a feature; it's the default for half the user base.

## Design Priorities

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Existing tokens target 4.5:1 body text contrast on all surface/bg pairings. Keyboard navigation and visible focus rings required. Reduced-motion alternatives required for all transitions.
